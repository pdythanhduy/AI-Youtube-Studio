## 5. Legal Risks

### Risk 1: CC BY-SA ShareAlike Clause (HIGH risk for commercial channels)
**Affected candidates:** C001-A, C008-A, C008-B, C008-C, C020-A, C020-B, C024-A (all CC BY-SA)
**Risk:** If any of these images are color-graded, the resulting video may be considered a derivative work requiring CC BY-SA licensing — incompatible with commercial YouTube monetization.
**Mitigation:** Either (a) use only CC BY / PD candidates for color-graded scenes, or (b) obtain paid stock licenses (Getty/PIXTA), or (c) seek legal advice on whether incorporating CC BY-SA images into a larger editorial work triggers SA.

### Risk 2: Resolution Limitations (MEDIUM — production quality risk)
**Affected:** C024-C (522×248 pixels — too low for full-frame use)
**Risk:** Cannot fill 1920×1080 without degradation.
**Mitigation:** Use C024-C only as a brief archival flash with grain overlay, not as primary final shot.

### Risk 3: Attribution Omission (LOW — manageable)
**Risk:** If CC BY images are used without attribution in video description, license is violated.
**Mitigation:** See `license_notes.md` Section 3 for required attribution text per candidate. Add all attributions to video description before publishing.

### Risk 4: Japan Govt Attribution Text Specificity (LOW)
**Affected:** C007-B, C024-B
**Risk:** The required attribution text is precise and long. If shortened or paraphrased, it may not satisfy the license requirement.
**Mitigation:** Copy the exact attribution string from `license_notes.md` Section 3. Do not paraphrase.
