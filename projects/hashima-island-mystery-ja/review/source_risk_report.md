## Critical Source: S4 — UNESCO WHC 39th Session

This is the single most legally and diplomatically sensitive source in the project. The claim that Japan's representative said "against their will" in the UNESCO session is cited in the script and description, and it directly implies Japanese government acknowledgment of forced labor.

**What the document says:** At the 39th session of the UNESCO World Heritage Committee (June/July 2015), Japan's representative stated: "Japan is prepared to take measures that allow an understanding that there were a large number of Koreans and others who were brought against their will and forced to work in harsh conditions in the 1940s at some of the sites."

**What the script says:**
```
日本政府代表は、一部の施設において人々が「意に反して（against their will）」労働させられたことに言及しました。
```

**Assessment:** The script's paraphrase is accurate to the key phrase "against their will" / 「意に反して」. However, the Japan government subsequently clarified that this acknowledgment did not constitute a legal admission of "forced labor" (強制労働) in the ILO definition sense, and pointed out that "forced to work" in the original English statement was meant to convey "obliged to work" rather than "physically coerced." The Japanese-language script uses 「意に反して……労働させられた」which is closer to the original English than the disputed Japanese interpretation.

**Risk assessment:** The script's treatment is accurate to the English primary source (UNESCO document in English). It does not overstate or understate the claim. However, this is precisely the phrase that triggered the Japan-South Korea diplomatic dispute afterward. The script correctly notes the subsequent controversy: 「その後、その発言の解釈をめぐって、日本と韓国の間で外交的な論争が生じています。」

**Verdict:** The source handling on S4 is acceptable. However, the production team must locate and retain the original UNESCO document for their records before publishing, in case of viewer disputes or platform content review.
